Did you know...?

Nearly 90% of what we throw away could potentially be recovered through reuse, recycling or composting

In New York, recycling is easy!

About a third of the waste produced can be recycled through the City's curbside recycling program.

Another third can be recovered through the City's organics programs

And another 10% (textiles, electronic waste, harmful household products, and plastic bags) can be diverted through donations and take-back programs!

More than 3,000 recycling bins are located across the City's five boroughs.

What to put in public recycling bins? In green bins, recycle newspapers, magazines, and mixed paper. In blue bins, recycle metal, glass bottles and jars, rigid plastic containers, and food and beverage cartons.

How about compositing bins? Compositing bins will help you recycle organic materials. It is helpful to classify organic materials in greens and browns. Greens are fresh, moist, plant materials (e.g. fruits and vegetables scraps, coffee grounds, tea bags, fresh leaves, grass clippings, etc). Browns are dry plan material (fall leaves, shredded paper, straw, wood chips, twigs, etc).
